# Korean Grammar Point: ~기 시작하다 [gi sijakhada] (Begin to)
## 1. Introduction
The grammar point ~기 시작하다 is a common expression in Korean that means "to begin to do something." It indicates the commencement of an action or process. This structure is particularly useful for conveying changes in states or actions that have just started.
---
## 2. Core Grammar Explanation
### Detailed Explanation
The grammar structure ~기 시작하다 consists of two parts:
- **~기**: The nominalizer that turns a verb into a noun.
- **시작하다**: This verb means "to begin."
### Structure
To use ~기 시작하다, follow this structure:
1. Start with the verb stem.
2. Add **-기** to the verb stem.
3. Follow with **시작하다**.
### Formation Diagram
```
Verb Stem + 기 시작하다
```
**Example:**
- **가다 (gada - to go) → 가기 시작하다 (gagi sijakhada - begin to go)**
---
## 3. Comparative Analysis
The structure ~기 시작하다 is often compared with other similar expressions that convey the idea of starting an action. A few notable comparisons include:
| Grammar Point          | Meaning                  | Usage Example                     |
|------------------------|--------------------------|-----------------------------------|
| ~기 시작하다          | Begin to do something    | 공부하기 시작하다 (begin to study)  |
| ~기 전에               | Before doing something   | 가기 전에 준비하다 (prepare before going) |
| ~고 있다               | Currently doing          | 공부하고 있다 (am studying)          |
### Differences:
- **~기 시작하다** emphasizes the start of an action, while **~고 있다** describes ongoing actions.
---
## 4. Examples in Context
### Sentence Examples
1. **Formal:**
   - 이제 공부하기 시작했습니다.  
   (I have begun to study now.)
  
2. **Informal:**
   - 나는 영화 보기 시작했어.  
   (I started to watch the movie.)
3. **Spoken:**
   - 요즘 운동하기 시작했어.  
   (I’ve started exercising these days.)
4. **Written:**
   - 그들은 새로운 프로젝트를 진행하기 시작했다.  
   (They began to undertake a new project.)
---
## 5. Cultural Notes
### Cultural Relevance
In Korean culture, the initiation of actions is significant as it often reflects a person's commitment or intention. The act of "starting" is more than just an action; it often signifies resolve and determination.
### Levels of Politeness and Formality
- When using ~기 시작하다, it's important to match the level of politeness and formality based on the context and the person you are speaking to.
### Idiomatic Expressions
Common phrases using this grammar include:
- 공부하기 시작하다 (begin to study)
- 운동하기 시작하다 (begin to exercise)
---
## 6. Common Mistakes and Tips
### Error Analysis
1. **Omitting the verb stem:** Remember to add **-기** after the verb stem before attaching **시작하다**.
   - Incorrect: *가 시작하다* → Correct: *가기 시작하다* (to begin to go)
2. **Inappropriate context use:** Be mindful of using this expression in contexts where someone has already begun the action as it might confuse listeners.
### Learning Strategies
- **Mnemonic Device:** Think of "G" for "Go" and "Start", linking with **기 시작하다** to remember it means "Begin to do".
---
## 7. Summary and Review
### Key Takeaways
- ~기 시작하다 means "to begin to do something."
- It is formed by adding **-기** to the verb stem followed by **시작하다**.
- Context matters in its usage for politeness forms in Korean.

## 1. Introduction
The grammar structure ~기 시작하다 is commonly used in the Korean language to express the idea of "beginning" to do something. It helps to indicate the initiation of an action or a state.
## 2. Core Grammar Explanation
### Detailed Explanation
- **Meaning**: ~기 시작하다 conveys that someone starts to perform an action or that a new situation begins. It is often used to highlight the onset of an activity or change.
- **Structure**: The structure is formed by taking the verb stem, adding ~기 and then finishing with 시작하다 (to begin).
### Formation Diagram
```
Verb Stem + 기 시작하다
```
### Example:
- **Verb Stem**: 읽다 (to read)
- **Formation**: 읽기 시작하다 (to begin to read)
## 3. Comparative Analysis
### Comparison with Similar Grammar Points
- **~기 때문이다**: This means "because" and is used to explain reasons, while ~기 시작하다 strictly indicates the start of an action.
- **~려고 하다**: This means "to intend to" and refers to plans or intentions rather than the actual beginning of the action.
### Comparison Table
| Grammar Point      | Meaning           | Usage Example                    |
|--------------------|-------------------|----------------------------------|
| ~기 시작하다       | Begin to          | 책을 읽기 시작했어요. (I began to read the book.) |
| ~기 때문이다       | Because           | 내일은 일요일이기 때문이에요. (It's because tomorrow is Sunday.) |
| ~려고 하다         | Intend to         | 영화를 보려고 해요. (I intend to watch a movie.) |
## 4. Examples in Context
### Sentence Examples
1. **Formal**: 
   - 한국어를 배우기 시작했습니다. 
   - (I began to learn Korean.)
  
2. **Informal**:

   - (I started doing yoga too.)
  
3. **Spoken**: 
   - 또 책 읽기 시작했어? 
   - (Did you start reading books again?)
4. **Written**: 
   - 우리는 새로운 프로젝트를 시작하기 위해 준비하기 시작했다.
   - (We began preparing to start the new project.)

### Levels of Politeness

### Idiomatic Expressions
- "시작이 반이다." - (Starting is half the task.) - This expression emphasizes the importance of initiating an action.
## 6. Common Mistakes and Tips

2. **Wrong Verb Stems**: Incorrectly using an unrelated verb stem can result in misunderstanding.
### Learning Strategies
- **Mnemonics**: Associate "시작" (begin/start) with events like a "starting gun" in races to remember that it denotes starting an action.
